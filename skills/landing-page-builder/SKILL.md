# Landing Page Builder Skill

## Purpose

This skill governs the generation of high-converting landing pages across four distinct funnel categories: **Low-Ticket Funnel**, **VSL Page**, **Opt-In Page**, and **Webinar Page**. It is derived from direct mobile-first analysis of 13 top-performing pages across these categories.

When generating a landing page, Claude must:
1. Identify the page category from the user's brief.
2. Follow the corresponding SOP below for component order and required elements.
3. Apply the copywriting skill (business-funding, direct-response, or credit-repair) for all written copy.
4. Leave typography, color palette, and decorative styling to industry best practices — do not prescribe them.
5. **Design mobile-first.** The 375px viewport is the primary canvas. Desktop is an enhancement, not the baseline.

---

## Universal Mobile-First Design Rules

These rules apply to every page category without exception.

- **Single-column layout on mobile.** No side-by-side columns below 768px.
- **Minimum tap target size of 48px** for all buttons and interactive elements.
- **Above-the-fold content must fit within 812px height** (iPhone viewport). The headline, subheadline, and primary CTA must all be visible without scrolling.
- **Font sizes:** Headline minimum 28px on mobile. Body text minimum 16px. Never smaller.
- **CTA buttons** must be full-width on mobile (100% container width), with high-contrast background and bold white text.
- **Video embeds** must use a 16:9 aspect ratio container that scales fluidly.
- **Sticky elements** (e.g., sticky CTA bar at bottom) are permitted and encouraged for long-form pages.
- **Spacing between sections** should be generous — 48px to 80px vertical padding per section — to create visual breathing room and clear separation.

## SOP 1: Low-Ticket Funnel Page

**Use when:** Selling a digital product, template pack, playbook, or course priced under $200.

**Core objective:** Maximize impulse purchase decisions. The page must create urgency, stack perceived value, and make the price feel like a no-brainer.

### Required Elements (in mobile scroll order)

| # | Element | Notes |
|---|---------|-------|
| 1 | **Urgency Bar** | Full-width top bar with countdown timer or limited-time offer copy. High contrast (e.g., dark background, bright text). |
| 2 | **Eyebrow Copy** | 1 line. Qualifies the reader. E.g., "For eCom brand owners who want to scale faster." |
| 3 | **Main Headline** | Bold, specific, outcome-driven. Include a number or timeframe where possible. |
| 4 | **Subheadline** | 1–2 sentences expanding on the headline. Who it's for and what they get. |
| 5 | **Primary CTA Button** | Full-width. Appears above the fold. Action-oriented copy (e.g., "Get Instant Access"). |
| 6 | **Feature Bullet List** | 4–6 bullets with icons. What's included. Quantity + specificity (e.g., "500+ pre-tested hooks"). |
| 7 | **Trust Bar / Logo Strip** | Client logos or "As seen in" logos. Grayscale or white on dark background. |
| 8 | **Problem Section** | Short headline naming the pain. 2–3 sentences of agitation. No solution yet. |
| 9 | **Solution Bridge** | Transition from problem to product. "That's why we built..." |
| 10 | **Product Breakdown** | Numbered steps or feature sections. Each feature gets a mini-headline + 1–2 sentence description. |
| 11 | **Social Proof Block** | 3–5 written testimonials with name, title/role. Star ratings optional. |
| 12 | **Offer / Pricing Box** | Product name, original price (strikethrough), sale price, bullet list of what's included, CTA button. |
| 13 | **Upsell / Bundle Option** | Optional second offer box below the primary. Higher price, more value. |
| 14 | **Guarantee Badge** | 14–30 day money-back guarantee. Bold headline + short description. |
| 15 | **Authority / Credibility Stats** | 3–4 bold statistics proving results (e.g., "$400MM+ generated for clients"). |
| 16 | **Case Studies** | 3–5 one-line result statements (e.g., "From $10k/month to $1.3M/month in 10 months"). |
| 17 | **FAQ Section** | 5–8 Q&A pairs addressing objections. Accordion-style on mobile. |
| 18 | **Final CTA Block** | Repeat the offer box or a simplified version. Last chance headline + CTA button. |

### Key Patterns Observed Across All Low-Ticket Pages
- Countdown timers and urgency language appear **above the fold on every page** — this is non-negotiable for this category.
- Feature bullet lists with specific quantities (numbers, counts) dramatically outperform vague benefit statements.
- The offer box appears **twice** on long pages — once in the middle and once at the bottom.
- Social proof uses role/title attribution (CMO, Co-Founder, CEO) to add credibility weight.
- Problem/solution framing is explicit and named — the page literally says "Here's the problem."
- Authority stats (revenue numbers, client counts) are displayed as large, bold numbers in their own section.

## SOP 2: VSL Page

**Use when:** The primary conversion mechanism is a video sales letter. The page exists to get the visitor to watch the video and then take action.

**Core objective:** Remove all distractions. Drive the visitor to watch the full video. The copy before and after the video supports the video — it does not replace it.

### Required Elements (in mobile scroll order)

| # | Element | Notes |
|---|---------|-------|
| 1 | **Eyebrow Copy** | Qualifies the audience immediately. E.g., "ONLY FOR: Coaches, Consultants & Agency Owners doing $10k+/month." |
| 2 | **Main Headline** | Bold guarantee or outcome statement. The headline must make a specific, believable promise. |
| 3 | **Subheadline** | Adds specificity or a timeframe to the headline promise. |
| 4 | **VSL Video Player** | Full-width on mobile. Autoplay muted or click-to-play. No other content competes with it above the fold. |
| 5 | **Primary CTA Button** | Appears immediately below the video. Revealed after a time delay or always visible depending on strategy. |
| 6 | **Social Proof Ticker / Star Ratings** | Scrolling testimonial ticker or star rating block directly below the CTA. Builds trust at the moment of decision. |
| 7 | **Trust Bar** | Client logos or media mentions. Positioned early to establish legitimacy. |
| 8 | **Problem Agitation Section** | Conversational copy naming the reader's pain. Direct, almost confrontational tone. |
| 9 | **Solution Introduction** | Introduce the offer/service as the logical answer to the problem. |
| 10 | **Process / How It Works** | 3-step or numbered breakdown of how the service/product works. Simple and scannable. |
| 11 | **Deep Social Proof** | Full testimonials with photos, names, and specific results. Video testimonials if available. |
| 12 | **Case Studies / Results** | Specific client outcomes with before/after framing. |
| 13 | **About / Authority Section** | Brief bio or company credentials. Why you are qualified to deliver this result. |
| 14 | **Offer Summary** | What they get, what it costs, what happens next. |
| 15 | **Guarantee** | Strong risk reversal. The stronger the guarantee, the better. |
| 16 | **FAQ** | 5–8 objection-handling Q&As. |
| 17 | **Final CTA** | Repeat the CTA with urgency. Scarcity copy (limited spots, deadline). |

### Key Patterns Observed Across All VSL Pages
- The video is the hero. **Nothing competes with it above the fold.** No sidebars, no feature lists, no distractions.
- Eyebrow copy is used aggressively to qualify and exclude — this is intentional. Unqualified visitors are a cost, not an opportunity.
- King Kong style: bold guarantee in the headline itself ("WE GUARANTEE TO BEAT YOUR CURRENT RESULTS OR YOU DON'T PAY"). This pattern converts because it leads with risk reversal before the ask.
- Scarcity language ("FREE spots are almost gone", "limited availability") appears near every CTA.
- Pages in this category are long — 15–25 scroll lengths on mobile is normal. The length is justified by the depth of social proof and objection handling.
- Minimal or no navigation. The page is a funnel, not a website.

## SOP 3: Opt-In Page

**Use when:** The goal is to capture an email address or phone number in exchange for a free lead magnet, training, challenge, or resource.

**Core objective:** Maximize opt-in rate. The page must be short, clear, and make the free offer feel irresistible. Friction must be near zero.

### Required Elements (in mobile scroll order)

| # | Element | Notes |
|---|---------|-------|
| 1 | **Profile Photo Row + Social Count** | Row of small circular profile photos with a count (e.g., "22,000+ members"). Builds instant social proof before a word is read. |
| 2 | **Eyebrow Copy** | Audience qualifier. "Attention [specific person] who wants [specific outcome]." |
| 3 | **Main Headline** | The most important element on the page. Must name the specific outcome or transformation. Curiosity or specificity — pick one and maximize it. |
| 4 | **Subheadline** | Reinforces the headline. Adds a timeframe, a mechanism, or a "without" statement (e.g., "without giving up carbs"). |
| 5 | **Benefit Bullet List** | 3–5 checkmark bullets. What they will learn/get. Specific and outcome-focused. |
| 6 | **Lead Magnet Visual** | Image or mockup of the free resource (ebook cover, video thumbnail, checklist preview). |
| 7 | **Opt-In Form** | First name + email (maximum). Phone optional for high-intent offers. CTA button copy must be benefit-driven, not generic ("Send Me The Free Training" not "Submit"). |
| 8 | **Countdown Timer** | Optional but effective. Creates urgency for the free offer. |
| 9 | **Social Proof** | Star ratings, testimonials, or before/after images. Positioned below the form for visitors who don't convert immediately. |
| 10 | **About / Authority** | Brief 2–3 sentence credibility statement. Who you are and why you can deliver this result. |
| 11 | **Second CTA** | Repeat the opt-in form or a button linking back to it. For longer pages only. |
| 12 | **Privacy / Disclaimer** | Small text below the form. "We respect your privacy. Unsubscribe anytime." Reduces form abandonment. |

### Key Patterns Observed Across All Opt-In Pages
- **Profile photo rows are the #1 above-the-fold trust signal** on opt-in pages. They appear on every high-performing example analyzed.
- The opt-in form appears **above the fold on mobile** on the best-performing pages. If the visitor has to scroll to find the form, conversion rate drops.
- Countdown timers on free offers create urgency that feels earned — "this free training is only available for X more minutes" is more believable than a fake scarcity timer on a paid product.
- Benefit bullets use checkmarks (✓) rather than standard bullet points — a small detail that signals "you will get these things."
- Pages in this category are intentionally short. The goal is a single conversion action. Long-form copy is a distraction here.
- Personal phone numbers and support emails in the header (as seen on clientsforfree.com) dramatically increase trust and perceived accessibility.

## SOP 4: Webinar / Challenge Page

**Use when:** Registering attendees for a live or automated webinar, challenge, or multi-day event.

**Core objective:** Get the registration. The page must sell the event itself — the transformation available inside the event — not the product being sold inside the event.

### Required Elements (in mobile scroll order)

| # | Element | Notes |
|---|---------|-------|
| 1 | **Event Date/Time Banner** | Full-width banner with the live event date, time, and timezone. Creates immediacy. E.g., "LIVE EVENT STARTING MONDAY, MARCH 2ND AT 5PM EST." |
| 2 | **Eyebrow Copy** | Who this event is for. Specific audience qualifier. |
| 3 | **Main Headline** | The transformation available inside the event. Specific outcome + timeframe. |
| 4 | **Subheadline** | The mechanism or the "how." What makes this different from what they've tried before. |
| 5 | **Event Preview / Video** | Short teaser video or event image. Gives a taste of what's inside. |
| 6 | **Star Ratings + Review Count** | Social proof of past event attendees. Positioned near the top. |
| 7 | **Trust Bar** | Logos of media mentions or partner brands. |
| 8 | **Price Display** | Original price (strikethrough) + current price. Even for low-cost events, showing a discount increases perceived value. |
| 9 | **Countdown Timer** | Time until the event starts. Creates hard deadline urgency. |
| 10 | **Registration Form / CTA** | Name + email. CTA button: "Reserve My Spot" or "Register Now." |
| 11 | **What You'll Learn** | 3–5 specific outcomes from attending. Bullet list with bold lead-ins. |
| 12 | **Who This Is For** | Explicit audience description. Helps self-qualification and increases show-up rate. |
| 13 | **About the Host** | Photo, name, credentials, and 2–3 proof points. Why they are the right person to teach this. |
| 14 | **Testimonials from Past Attendees** | Specific results from previous events or programs. |
| 15 | **FAQ** | Address: "Is this live or recorded?", "What if I can't attend live?", "What do I need to participate?" |
| 16 | **Final Registration CTA** | Repeat the form or button with urgency copy. "Spots are limited. Register before [date]." |

### Key Patterns Observed Across Webinar Pages
- The event date and time is the **first thing visible** — this is the primary urgency mechanism and must be treated as such.
- Price anchoring (showing a higher original price crossed out) is used even for low-cost or free events. This increases perceived value of the registration itself.
- Countdown timers on webinar pages count down to the event start — this is a hard, real deadline, which makes the urgency more credible than artificial scarcity.
- Star ratings and review counts from past attendees appear near the top, before the registration form. Social proof of the event experience, not just the outcome.
- "Who this is for" sections serve a dual purpose: they qualify the right registrants and increase show-up rate by making attendees feel the event was made specifically for them.

## Skill Usage Instructions

When this skill is loaded, Claude must:

1. **Ask for the page category** if not specified in the brief: Low-Ticket, VSL, Opt-In, or Webinar.
2. **Follow the SOP** for that category — every required element must be present in the output.
3. **Apply the copywriting skill** for all written copy. Do not write generic placeholder copy.
4. **Output Puck-compatible JSON** using the component names in the Component Reference Library above.
5. **Do not add navigation menus** unless explicitly requested. These are conversion pages, not websites.
6. **Do not skip the guarantee or FAQ** — these are objection-handling elements that directly impact conversion rate.
