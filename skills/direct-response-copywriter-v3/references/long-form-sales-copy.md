# Long-Form Sales Page / TSL Copywriting Skill

This skill provides the framework for writing comprehensive, high-persuasion long-form sales pages or Text Sales Letters (TSLs). This format is designed to take a cold or warm prospect through the entire buyer's journey, from problem awareness to purchase, in a single, continuous reading experience.

---

## 1. Data Ingestion Protocol

1.  **Deep Dive into Sales Call Transcripts:** Go beyond surface-level pain points. Extract detailed stories, metaphors, and the emotional journey customers describe. Identify the core belief systems that need to be dismantled and rebuilt.
2.  **Deconstruct Winning Sales Pages: Analyze the provided Sabri Suby sales copy (`sell_like_crazy_sales_copy.txt`) as a primary example of a high-converting long-form sales letter. Pay close attention to the headline structure, the origin story, the use of proof, and the overall tone.** Analyze the logical flow and argument structure of provided examples. Map out the sequence of headlines, subheadlines, transitions, and closes. Note how proof and story are woven together.

## 2. Long-Form Sales Page Framework

A long-form sales page is a meticulously constructed argument. It must be both skimmable (via headlines) and deeply persuasive for those who read every word.

### Part 1: The Headline & Opening

-   **The Headline & Opening Hook:** The opening must make a huge promise, create a massive curiosity gap, or call out the specific audience. For creative inspiration on opener templates and transition language, consult the swipe file at `/home/ubuntu/skills/copywriter/references/swipe_file.md`. The following sections are worth drawing from — use the underlying idea or energy, not the exact words:

    *   **Section 7: Assumptive Questions Transition Template**
    *   **Section 9: Opener Template — "Their Stuff Is Great IF" Competitive Positioning**
    *   **Section 10: Full VSL Script — Performance Coaching Niche (Long-Form)** (use as a structural reference, not a template to fill in)

### Part 2: Problem Agitation & The Origin Story

-   **Intensify the Pain:** Go deep into the consequences of the problem. Use the exact language and stories from your transcript analysis to make the prospect feel seen and understood.
-   **Introduce the Origin Story:** Tell the story of the product's creator or a central character who struggled with the same problem, establishing them as a reluctant but credible guide. (Reference: Playbook 3.1)

### Part 3: The Unique Mechanism Reveal

-   **The Turning Point:** In the origin story, describe the "aha" moment where the solution was discovered.
-   **Introduce and Explain the Mechanism:** This is the core of the sales argument. Name your Unique Mechanism and explain the Force of Nature it harnesses. This section must logically prove *why* your solution works where others have failed. (Reference: `unique_mechanism.md`)
-   **Apply Declarative Knowledge:** Explain the mechanism conceptually — what it is, how it works in principle, what outcomes it produces, and what it makes possible. Do not give the reader a step-by-step guide to implementing it themselves. The copy should create understanding and desire, not a DIY roadmap. The "how to actually do it" is what the product delivers.

### Part 4: The Solution & Proof

-   **Introduce the Product:** Formally introduce your product as the embodiment of the Unique Mechanism.
-   **Show, Don't Just Tell:** Provide overwhelming proof that the solution works. This includes testimonials, case studies, data, demonstrations, and social proof.

### Part 5: The Offer Stack & Close

-   **Build the Offer:** Stack all the components of your offer, assigning a high monetary value to each to build immense perceived value.
-   **The Price Drop & Justification:** Reveal the total value, then reveal the much lower actual price. Justify the price using a framework like "Cost of Inaction." (Reference: Playbook 4.2)
-   **Risk Reversal:** Introduce a powerful guarantee to remove all risk for the buyer.
-   **The Final Close:** Use a strong closing framework to compel immediate action. The "Two Futures" or "Identity Alignment" closes are particularly effective here. (Reference: Playbook 1.6, 4.4)
-   **P.S. Section:** Add one or more postscripts to summarize the offer, add urgency, or handle a final key objection.

## 3. Self-Revision Checklist

-   [ ] **Headline & Skim Test:** Can a reader understand the core argument and promise just by reading the headlines and subheadlines?
-   [ ] **Logical Flow:** Does the argument progress logically and persuasively from problem to solution without any gaps?
-   [ ] **Mechanism as Centerpiece:** Is the Unique Mechanism the undeniable hero of the story and the core reason the solution works?
-   [ ] **Declarative Knowledge:** Is the mechanism explained conceptually (what it is, how it works, what it produces) without giving away step-by-step implementation instructions?
-   [ ] **Overwhelming Proof:** Is the page loaded with enough proof (testimonials, case studies, data) to make the claims undeniable?
-   [ ] **Irresistible Offer:** Is the offer stack so compelling that the price seems like a bargain?
-   [ ] **Powerful Close:** Does the close use a specific, proven framework to drive a decision?
-   [ ] **Readability:** Is the text broken up with subheadings, bolding, bullet points, and images to make it easy to read?
-   [ ] **No Forced Negatives:** Has the copy avoided the "It's not X, it's Y" pattern entirely? Zero instances allowed.
-   [ ] **Simplicity & Clarity:** Can anyone read this page and immediately understand what is being offered and exactly why they should take action? If there is any moment of confusion, rewrite it.
-   [ ] **No Staccato Fragments:** Has the copy avoided a string of overly short, choppy sentences? Combine related ideas into single, flowing sentences. Go from "No skills needed. No experience. No special education." to "No skills, experience, or special education needed."
