# Ad Script Copywriting Skill

This skill provides the specific framework and workflow for creating high-converting direct response ad scripts. Ad scripts are written to be spoken by a person and must be highly conversational.

---

## 1. Data Ingestion Protocol

Before writing the script, you MUST perform the following data analysis:

1.  **Analyze Sales Call Transcripts:**
    *   Read through the provided sales call transcript summaries.
    *   Identify and extract the top 5-10 most common and emotionally charged **pain points**, **objections**, and **desire statements**.
    *   Pay close attention to the exact phrasing the customers use. You will use this verbatim language in the script.

2.  **Analyze Winning Ads:**
    *   You MUST read and internalize the winning ad examples and analysis located in `/home/ubuntu/skills/copywriter/references/winning_ads.md`.
    *   Model all new ad scripts on the hooks, angles, and the "Catalyst Formula" structure identified in the analysis section of that file.

## 2. Ad Script Framework (Hook -> Problem -> Mechanism -> CTA)

All ad scripts MUST follow this general structure. For inspiration on language and angles, consult the swipe file at `/home/ubuntu/skills/copywriter/references/swipe_file.md`. Use these templates as a creative springboard — adapt and riff on them, do not copy them verbatim.

### Part 1: The Hook (First 3-5 Seconds)

Your single most important job is to stop the scroll. The hook must create a powerful pattern interrupt and open a curiosity loop. Model your hooks on the patterns in `winning_ads.md`. For additional creative inspiration, the following swipe file sections contain proven hook patterns worth drawing from:

*   **Section 12: Opener Template — "Dirty Secret the Gurus Won't Tell You"**
*   **Section 14: Opener Template — "You Should Give Up"**
*   **Section 15: Opener Template — "Secret Reputation"**
*   **Section 21: Hook — "If You're a ______" Call-Reluctant Coach"**

These are reference points, not scripts to follow. Use the underlying idea or structure and write something fresh.

### Part 2: The Problem (Seconds 5-15)

Quickly agitate the core problem, using the prospect's own language.

*   **Use Diagnostic Framing:** Describe the problem's symptoms with such accuracy that the prospect feels you are reading their mind. (Reference: Playbook 1.4)
*   **Externalize the Blame:** Frame the problem as being caused by an external factor, not their own fault. This removes shame and makes them receptive to a solution. (Reference: Playbook 1.1)
*   **Use Verbatim Language:** Inject the exact pain point phrases you extracted from the sales call transcripts.

### Part 3: The Mechanism Reveal (Seconds 15-45)

Introduce your Unique Mechanism as the solution to the problem.

*   **Name the Mechanism:** Give your proprietary process a name (e.g., "The Client Attraction Funnel").
*   **Explain How It Works:** Briefly explain the core components of the mechanism. **Consult the Mechanism Introduction Library in `winning_ads.md` for inspiration on how to introduce the Client Attraction Funnel.** Select an introduction that matches the ad's primary angle (e.g., systems, lead quality, simplicity).
*   **Connect to the Result:** Clearly state how the mechanism delivers the desired outcome.

### Part 4: The Call-to-Action (CTA) (Final 10-15 Seconds)

Tell the prospect exactly what to do next.

*   **Be Clear and Direct:** Use a single, unambiguous call-to-action (e.g., "Click the link below," "Book a call now").
*   **Use a Decision Frame:** Frame the CTA as a choice between two futures: one with their problem solved, and one without. (Reference: Playbook 4.4)
*   **Stack the Desire:** Briefly restate the core promise and the ultimate desire the prospect wants to achieve.

## 3. Self-Revision Checklist

Before delivering the final script, you MUST audit your draft against this checklist:

-   [ ] **Hook Potency:** Does the hook make a specific, falsifiable claim and create a powerful curiosity gap in the first 3 seconds?
-   [ ] **Conversational Tone:** Does the script sound like a real person talking to a friend? Is the grammar and pacing natural for spoken word?
-   [ ] **Prospect Language:** Is the problem section built using the exact pain point language from the sales call transcripts?
-   [ ] **Mechanism Clarity:** Is the Unique Mechanism clearly named and explained (Mechanism + Force + Result)?
-   [ ] **Single CTA:** Is there one, and only one, clear call-to-action?
-   [ ] **Correct Personalization:** Is the problem described in the third person, and the solution in the second person ("you")?
-   [ ] **Forward Momentum:** Does every line of the script create a desire to hear the next line?
-   [ ] **No Forced Negatives:** Has the copy avoided the "It's not X, it's Y" pattern entirely? Zero instances allowed.
-   [ ] **Simplicity & Clarity:** Can anyone listen to this script and immediately understand what is being offered and exactly why they should take action? If there is any moment of confusion, rewrite it.
-   [ ] **No Staccato Fragments:** Has the copy avoided a string of overly short, choppy sentences? Combine related ideas into single, flowing sentences. Go from "No skills needed. No experience. No special education." to "No skills, experience, or special education needed."
