# Bullet Copywriting Skill

This skill provides the specific framework and workflow for creating high-converting direct response bullet points (also known as fascinations). Bullets are used to build curiosity, convey benefits, and drive the prospect deeper into the sales message.

---

## 1. Core Principles of Bullet Writing

Before writing, you MUST internalize these core principles, synthesized from the "21 Bullet Secrets" PDF:

1.  **Curiosity is King:** The primary job of a bullet is to make the prospect desperate to know the answer. It should open a loop that can only be closed by reading on or buying the product.
2.  **Specificity Builds Credibility:** Vague promises are weak. Specific numbers, names, and mechanisms make the benefit feel real and achievable.
3.  **Vary Your Formulas:** Do not use the same bullet formula repeatedly. Mix and match from the formula library below to keep the copy fresh and engaging.
4.  **Lead with Intrigue, Follow with Benefit:** The best bullets combine a curiosity-piquing idea with a clear, desirable benefit.
5.  **Write More Than You Need:** The best practice is to write 5-7x more bullets than you will actually use, then select only the most powerful ones for the final copy.

## 2. Bullet Writing Formulas (from "21 Bullet Secrets")

This is a library of proven bullet formulas. You MUST use a variety of these formulas when writing any bullet list.

| Formula | Description | Example (from Sabri Suby) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. The "How To" Bullet** | The most common formula. Promises to reveal a method for achieving a desired outcome. | "How to write Google AdWords or Facebook ads that practically FORCE your prospects to click them and buy!" |
| **2. The "Secret To" Bullet** | Used for information that is not commonly known. Implies an insider advantage. | "The secrets of \"Value Based Marketing\" ...This simple strategy gives you the power to break through and CRUSH much larger and more established competitors!" |
| **3. The "Why" Bullet** | Explains the reason behind a surprising or counter-intuitive fact, implying a benefit to knowing the answer. | "Why you could pay up to 5 times more money for the same amount of traffic if Google updates their algorithm — and why that's good news for YOU!" |
| **4. The "What" Bullet** | Gives specific instructions for action or reveals valuable, guarded information. | "What you absolutely MUST do immediately before setting up a website, landing page or running ads of ANY kind, to stop you from lighting your money on fire..." |
| **5. The "What NEVER" Bullet** | A powerful fear-based formula that warns the prospect away from a common mistake. | "Why you should never ever tell a prospect how you can help them! NEVER! And what to do that's 5 times more powerful..." |
| **6. The "Number" Bullet** | Groups multiple secrets, methods, or reasons into a single, high-value bullet. | "The 17 fastest sales-producing secrets known to man! (These go against everything you've ever been told by the 'Gurus')" |
| **7. The "Right... WRONG!" Bullet** | States a common belief and then immediately debunks it, creating a strong curiosity gap. | "Your business needs to rank on page #1 of Google so people can find you, right? WRONG! — Page 202 explains why." |
| **8. The "Gimmick" Bullet** | Assigns a creative, memorable name to a specific technique or strategy. | "How to use 'The Godfather Strategy' to craft irresistible offers, that practically FORCE your prospects to buy your products or services..." |
| **9. The "If... Then" Bullet** | Engages the prospect by having them meet a simple requirement, then promises a specific benefit. | (Not present in Sabri's copy, but a valid formula) |

## 3. Bullet Writing Workflow

1.  **Identify Core Benefits:** Review the offer and identify the top 10-20 key benefits, features, and unique ideas.
2.  **Generate Raw Bullets:** For each core benefit, write at least 3-5 different bullet variations using different formulas from the library above.
3.  **Inject Specificity & Intrigue:** Go back through the raw bullets and add specific numbers, names, and curiosity-piquing language (e.g., "The single best way...", "A little-known strategy...").
4.  **Model the Masters:** Compare your bullets to the examples from Sabri Suby in `sell_like_crazy_bullets.txt`. Are they as compelling? Do they create as much curiosity?
5.  **Select & Refine:** Choose the strongest 10-20% of the bullets you generated. Ensure they are varied in length and formula. Refine the wording for maximum impact.

## 4. Self-Revision Checklist

Before delivering the final bullet list, you MUST audit your draft against this checklist:

-   [ ] **Curiosity Gap:** Does each bullet make the prospect think, "I need to know the answer to that"?
-   [ ] **Benefit-Driven:** Is the underlying benefit of each bullet clear and desirable?
-   [ ] **Formula Variety:** Have at least 3-4 different bullet formulas been used in the list?
-   [ ] **Specificity:** Are the bullets specific and credible, or are they vague and full of hype?
-   [ ] **No Reveal:** Does the bullet tease the information without actually giving it away?
-   [ ] **Emotional Impact:** Do the bullets tap into core emotions like fear, greed, curiosity, or exclusivity?
-   [ ] **No Forced Negatives:** Has the copy avoided the "It's not X, it's Y" pattern entirely? Zero instances allowed.
-   [ ] **Simplicity & Clarity:** Can anyone read these bullets and immediately understand what is being teased and why it matters to them? If there is any moment of confusion, rewrite it.
-   [ ] **No Staccato Fragments:** Has the copy avoided a string of overly short, choppy sentences? Combine related ideas into single, flowing sentences. Go from "No skills needed. No experience. No special education." to "No skills, experience, or special education needed."
