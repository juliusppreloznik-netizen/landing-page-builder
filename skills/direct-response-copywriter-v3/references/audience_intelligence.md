## 1. Market Sophistication & Awareness Levels

This is the calibration layer for all copy. You MUST ask the user for the target sophistication and awareness level at the start of every session. Use these tables to guide your writing.

### Market Sophistication Level Guide

| Level | Who They Are | What They Respond To | What to Avoid |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level 1** | Brand new to the idea. Has never heard of a paid client acquisition system for credit repair. | Big, bold outcome claims. Lead with the dream. "Get 5-10 qualified leads per day on autopilot." | Complex mechanism explanations. They don't care how yet — they just want to know it's possible. |
| **Level 2** | Has heard of paid ads and funnels but has never tried them. Curious but uninformed. | The promise + a simple "how it works" overview. Lead with the outcome, then briefly explain the vehicle. | Jargon, acronyms, technical details. Keep it simple. |
| **Level 3** | Has tried ads or hired an agency once. Got mediocre results. Not deeply burned, just disappointed. | Acknowledge the past attempt. Explain why it didn't work (it was the wrong system, not their fault). Lead with differentiation. | Ignoring their past experience. They need to feel heard before they'll listen. |
| **Level 4** | Has been burned by 2-3 agencies. Spent real money. Got bad leads or no results. Deeply skeptical. | Proof first. Specificity. Mechanism differentiation. Calm, expert tone. No hype. Acknowledge their skepticism directly. | Big outcome claims without immediate proof. Excitement-based hooks. |
| **Level 5** | Has tried multiple agencies, courses, mentors. Exhausted. May be close to quitting. | Lead with empathy and a completely new angle. The mechanism must feel genuinely different from anything they've seen. Proof must be overwhelming. | Any language that sounds like what they've already heard. This level requires the most creative, contrarian approach. |

### Market Awareness Level Guide

| Level | Who They Are | What They Respond To |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Unaware** | Does not know they have a lead generation problem. Thinks their current situation is normal. | Lead with a pattern interrupt about their situation. Make them aware of the gap between where they are and where they could be. |
| **Problem Aware** | Knows they have a lead problem but doesn't know what the solution is. | Agitate the problem. Make the pain vivid. Then introduce the solution category. |
| **Solution Aware** | Knows solutions exist (ads, funnels, agencies) but hasn't found the right one. | Skip the problem. Lead with your mechanism and why it's different from what they've tried. |
| **Product Aware** | Knows about you specifically but hasn't bought yet. | Lead with proof, testimonials, and objection handling. Remove the last barriers to action. |
| **Most Aware** | Ready to buy. Just needs a reason to act now. | Lead with the offer, the guarantee, and urgency. Make it easy to say yes. |

## 2. Verbatim Pain Point & Desire Language

This is the raw material for all copy. You MUST pull language directly from these lists when writing about pain points and desires.

### Core Pain Points (Ranked by Frequency)

1.  **Inconsistent / Unpredictable Client Flow:**
    *   "I'm still at the mercy of them referring and then how many people that they know."
    *   "I get to a gray area once those two people's credit is clean and they don't know anyone else with bad credit."
    *   "I haven't been at a consistent number."
    *   "It was just nothing that was really consistent."
    *   "I'll have a run for like a month or two and then it'll just die out."
    *   "The clients aren't consistent because it's organic."

2.  **Failed Past Attempts with Other Agencies/Mentors:**
    *   "The last three ad companies weren't any good."
    *   "I feel like I'm just wasting money."
    *   "I was spending $70 a day and nobody was answering the calls."
    *   "I've gone through a few different agencies."
    *   "I had a mentor who didn't teach me to run ads the proper way."
    *   "I spent $3,000 on a mentorship and didn't learn anything."
    *   "I paid $5,000 for a one-on-one mentorship."
    *   "I've tried all this shit before and it just never panned out."

3.  **Low-Quality / Low-Intent Leads:**
    *   "Some of the leads that come through from content are not the most serious."
    *   "I was getting leads and calls were getting booked, but they weren't high-intent leads."
    *   "The leads were just shit."
    *   "The Instagram DMs are no good, literally no good."
    *   "The Instagram DMs are fucking garbage."

4.  **Overwhelm & Burnout:**
    *   "It was low key too much and I got burnt out."
    *   "I was burdened with the people that I was working on."
    *   "I was so bogged down and overwhelmed with the amount of people we had to process."
    *   "I'm having a problem with my contact form on my webpage."
    *   "I feel like I'm a little bit over my head."

5.  **Fear & Skepticism:**
    *   "I'm just being leery."
    *   "I think I'm just being scary."
    *   "I'm scared of wasting money."
    *   "I just don't want to run into that. I'll have to answer like questions on what I'm doing right now, you know what I mean?"

### Core Desires (Ranked by Frequency)

1.  **A Predictable, Automated System:**
    *   "I just wanted to get a more consistent deal flow, basically outside of referrals."
    *   "I want this to be automated."
    *   "I need something like, you know, go high level."
    *   "I want a system where I have somebody calling to confirm the appointment."
    *   "A system to get more leads."

2.  **Freedom & Full-Time Entrepreneurship:**
    *   "I would like to do this full time."
    *   "Once I get to a consistent number, the goal is to go into it full time and then hire another closer."
    *   "I want to be able to be completely independent."
    *   "I want an opportunity where I can just step away from my business in a sense with that sales team."

3.  **Higher Quality, Higher Ticket Clients:**
    *   "I'm trying to also incorporate the high ticket option as well."
    *   "I want the high ticket stuff where I could close at least a few deals a week and feel like this is making sense."
    *   "I wanted to start marketing, but be able to market at a higher price point."

4.  **Growth & Scalability:**
    *   "Then it'd be me and the closer, and then eventually just a closer."
    *   "We get a sales team in place."
    *   "I want to make $100k a month."

5.  **Done-For-You Help & Guidance:**
    *   "I want your team to send me scripts because I don't know what I'm doing."
    *   "We can have a partnership and you can kind of teach me even things that I don't know. Yeah, kind of point me in that right direction."
    *   "A 'done for you' service."
