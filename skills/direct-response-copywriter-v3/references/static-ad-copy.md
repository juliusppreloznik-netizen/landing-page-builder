# Static Ad Copywriting Skill

This skill provides the framework for creating static (text-based) direct response ads, such as those for Facebook, Google, or other platforms. The goal is to stop the scroll and drive a click with a powerful combination of a headline and body copy.

---

## 1. Data Ingestion Protocol

1.  **Analyze Sales Call Transcripts:** Extract the most potent, emotionally charged pain points, desires, and objections. Focus on short, punchy phrases used by customers.
2.  **Analyze Winning Ads:** Review provided examples of successful static ads. Deconstruct the headline formulas, body copy structure, and calls-to-action.

---

## 2. Static Ad Copy Framework

Static ads are composed of two primary parts: the Headline and the Body Copy, followed by a clear CTA.

### Part 1: The Headline

The headline is 80% of the ad. It must function as a complete persuasion unit on its own. Its only job is to get the prospect to read the first line of the body copy. For creative inspiration on headline formulas, consult the swipe file at `/home/ubuntu/skills/copywriter/references/swipe_file.md`. The following sections contain proven patterns worth drawing from — use the underlying structure, not the exact phrasing:

*   **Section 12: Opener Template — "Dirty Secret the Gurus Won’t Tell You"**
*   **Section 16: Headline Template — "The Ugly Truth About ______"**
*   **Section 17: Headline Template — "How to ______ Even If ______"**
*   **Section 20: Headline Template — "For ______ Who Want ______"**

### Part 2: The Body Copy

The body copy expands on the headline's promise and bridges to the call-to-action. It must be concise and highly readable.

*   **First Line:** The first sentence must immediately follow through on the headline's promise and create enough momentum to get the prospect to read the second line.
*   **Agitate the Problem:** Briefly touch on the core pain point, using the prospect's own language.
*   **Introduce the Mechanism:** Hint at or name the Unique Mechanism that makes the promised result possible. You don't need to explain it fully here; the goal is to create intrigue that drives the click.
*   **Bridge to CTA:** Smoothly transition to the call-to-action.

### Part 3: The Call-to-Action (CTA)

*   **Be Clear and Direct:** Tell the user exactly what to do (e.g., "Click 'Learn More' to see how it works," "Get the full story here").

## 3. Self-Revision Checklist

-   [ ] **Headline Power:** Does the headline use a proven bullet formula and contain a powerful hook?
-   [ ] **Readability:** Is the body copy easy to read, with short sentences and paragraphs?
-   [ ] **Continuity:** Does the first line of the body copy directly follow from the headline?
-   [ ] **Intrigue:** Does the copy create enough curiosity about the Unique Mechanism to compel a click?
-   [ ] **Clear CTA:** Is the call-to-action simple, direct, and unambiguous?
-   [ ] **Adherence to Core Principles:** Does the ad follow all the rules in `principles.md` (no forced negatives, correct personalization, etc.)?
-   [ ] **No Forced Negatives:** Has the copy avoided the "It's not X, it's Y" pattern entirely? Zero instances allowed.
-   [ ] **Simplicity & Clarity:** Can anyone read this ad and immediately understand what is being offered and exactly why they should take action? If there is any moment of confusion, rewrite it.
-   [ ] **No Staccato Fragments:** Has the copy avoided a string of overly short, choppy sentences? Combine related ideas into single, flowing sentences. Go from "No skills needed. No experience. No special education." to "No skills, experience, or special education needed."
