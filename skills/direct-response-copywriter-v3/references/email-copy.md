# Email Copywriter Skill

This skill provides the framework for writing high-converting daily emails, nurture sequences, and broadcast campaigns. It is built on the same Declarative Knowledge Doctrine as value assets, but with strict formatting rules for email pacing and readability.

---

## 1. The Declarative Knowledge Doctrine (Email Application)

Like all value assets, emails must teach **ABOUT** things, never **HOW** to do things. 

An email should shift a belief, expose a hidden problem, or introduce a new mechanism. It should never be a step-by-step tutorial. If the reader can implement the solution just by reading the email, you have removed their reason to book a call or buy.

**The Quick Win Exception:** You may provide procedural "how-to" knowledge in an email ONLY if it solves a micro-problem that immediately exposes a macro-problem. (e.g., "Here is the exact script to get a referral today... but once you get them on the phone, if your sales process is broken, you'll still lose the deal. Here's how we fix the sales process...")

## 2. Email Frameworks

When requested to write an email, use one of the following frameworks based on the goal.

### Type A: The Objection Handle Email
Designed to dismantle a specific objection before the prospect gets on a call.

1. **The Subject Line:** Curiosity-driven or directly calling out the objection (e.g., "Why referrals are killing your growth").
2. **The Hook:** Call out the objection directly using the prospect's exact verbatim language from `audience_intelligence.md`.
3. **The Pivot:** Explain why the premise of their objection is based on outdated or incorrect information.
4. **The Reframe:** Introduce the new reality or mechanism that renders the objection irrelevant.
5. **The Proof:** Provide a specific number, case study, or logical proof.
6. **The CTA:** Direct them to book a call or watch the VSL.

### Type B: The Indoctrination Email
Designed to tie a specific desire to your Unique Mechanism.

1. **The Subject Line:** Benefit-driven with a curiosity gap.
2. **The Hook:** State the desire and the massive gap between wanting it and having it.
3. **The Root Cause:** Clinically explain the hidden root problem causing them to fail.
4. **The Inevitable Conclusion:** Introduce your Unique Mechanism as the direct solution to that root cause.
5. **The Monopoly:** Position your mechanism as the ONLY logical way to get the desire.
6. **The CTA:** Tell them exactly how to get access to this mechanism.

### Type C: The "Story-Lesson-Pitch" Email (Seinfeld Style)
Designed for daily engagement and relationship building.

1. **The Subject Line:** Weird, specific, or story-based (e.g., "What the barista taught me about credit repair").
2. **The Story:** A brief, entertaining personal anecdote or observation (1-3 paragraphs).
3. **The Pivot:** A smooth transition connecting the story to a business principle.
4. **The Lesson:** The declarative insight or belief shift.
5. **The Pitch:** The CTA tied directly to the lesson.

## 3. Formatting & Pacing Rules (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

Email copy is consumed differently than any other medium. It must be visually effortless to read on a mobile device.

**Rule 1: Line-Break Pacing**
- NEVER write block paragraphs. 
- Use line breaks as a pacing instrument. Every line is its own beat.
- A short line lands the punch.
- A longer line builds the setup.
- Maximum 2-3 sentences per visual block before a hard return.

**Rule 2: Visual Breathing Room**
- Use ellipses (...) to create pauses.
- Use single-sentence paragraphs for emphasis.
- If a sentence looks dense, break it in half.

**Example of Correct Pacing:**
> Credit repair business owners, let me ask you something that's been bothering me.
> 
> There are 140 million people in America with poor credit right now. 140 million.
> 
> Even if just 1% of them needed credit repair badly enough to pay for it...
> 
> That's 1.4 million potential clients.
> 
> So why aren’t you at $30k/month yet?

## 4. Self-Revision Checklist

Before delivering the final email, you MUST audit your draft against this checklist:

- [ ] **Pacing Check:** Is the email formatted with hard line breaks? Are there any block paragraphs that need to be broken up?
- [ ] **Declarative Check:** Does the email teach *about* the concept without giving away the step-by-step implementation?
- [ ] **Audience Alignment:** Is the core desire or objection pulled directly from `audience_intelligence.md`?
- [ ] **Mechanism Integration:** Is the Unique Mechanism positioned as the logical solution?
- [ ] **Voice & Tone:** Does the copy match the specific voice fingerprint defined in `voice_model.md`?
- [ ] **Banned Patterns:** Have you scanned for and removed all patterns listed in `banned_patterns.md`?
- [ ] **Feature Translation:** Have all technical features been translated into plain-English outcomes per the Feature Translation Doctrine?
- [ ] **Clear CTA:** Does the email tell the prospect exactly what to do next?
