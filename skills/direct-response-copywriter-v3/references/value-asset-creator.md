# Value Asset Creator Skill

This skill provides the framework for creating high-converting educational content, including Lead Magnets (PDFs, checklists, mini-trainings) and Social Media Value Posts. The goal of these assets is to build authority, shift beliefs, and create intense desire for the core offer without giving away the farm.

---

## 1. The Declarative Knowledge Doctrine

This doctrine governs ALL educational content you create. It is the single most important rule for value assets.

**You teach ABOUT things. You never teach HOW to do things.**

Declarative knowledge is conceptual. It tells the prospect what something is, how it works in principle, what it produces, and what it makes possible. It creates understanding and desire without giving away the implementation. Procedural knowledge (step-by-step instructions) belongs inside the paid product.

The test is simple: after consuming this asset, could the prospect go implement the entire system on their own without buying? If yes, you have given away procedural knowledge. Rewrite it as declarative.

**The "Quick Win" Exception:**
The only time you provide procedural "how-to" knowledge in a value asset is for a **Quick Win**. A Quick Win solves one very narrow, specific micro-problem immediately. It proves you can help them by actually helping them. However, solving this micro-problem must naturally expose a much larger macro-problem that can only be solved by your core offer.

## 2. Asset Types & Frameworks

When the user requests a value asset, they will specify the type. Use the corresponding framework below.

### Type A: The Objection Handle Asset
This asset is designed to systematically dismantle a specific objection or technical obstacle before the prospect ever gets on a sales call.

**Creation Workflow:**
1. **Extraction:** Scan `audience_intelligence.md` and extract a specific, high-friction objection, obstacle, or technical question.
2. **The Hook:** Call out the objection directly using the prospect's exact verbatim language. Validate their frustration.
3. **The Pivot:** Explain why the premise of their objection is based on outdated or incorrect information (the "False Belief").
4. **The Reframe:** Introduce the new reality or mechanism that renders the objection irrelevant.
5. **The Proof:** Provide specific data, a case study, or logical proof that the new reality is true.
6. **The CTA:** Direct them to the next step in the funnel to implement this new reality.

### Type B: The Indoctrination Asset
This asset is designed to tie a specific desire to your Unique Mechanism, positioning your mechanism as the *only* logical vehicle to achieve that desire.

**Creation Workflow:**
1. **Extraction:** Scan `audience_intelligence.md` and extract a specific, high-priority business outcome or desire.
2. **The Hook:** State the desire and the massive gap between wanting it and having it.
3. **The Root Cause:** Clinically and logically explain the hidden root problem causing them to fail at achieving this desire. Make them feel understood.
4. **The Inevitable Conclusion:** Introduce your Unique Mechanism/Service Methodology. Explain *mechanically* how it directly neutralizes the root problem.
5. **The Monopoly:** Position your mechanism as the ONLY logical way to get the desire, because all other methods fail to address the root problem.
6. **The CTA:** Tell them exactly how to get access to this mechanism.

## 3. Format Guidelines

Value assets generally take one of two formats. Adapt the frameworks above to fit the requested format.

### Format 1: Social Media Value Posts (Facebook, LinkedIn, Instagram)
- **Structure:** Short, punchy, and highly readable. Use line breaks as a pacing tool.
- **Tone:** Conversational, authoritative, slightly contrarian.
- **Visuals:** Suggest specific visual assets (e.g., `[Visual: A simple 2-column comparison chart showing Old Way vs. New Way]`).
- **Length:** 300-600 words.

### Format 2: Lead Magnets (PDF Guides, Checklists, Cheat Sheets)
- **Structure:** Highly structured with clear headings, bullet points, and actionable (but declarative) takeaways.
- **The "Consumption" Goal:** The lead magnet must be easily consumable in under 10 minutes. Do not write a textbook.
- **The Bridge:** The final 20% of the lead magnet must serve as a bridge, transitioning from the "Quick Win" or "Declarative Insight" directly into a pitch for the core offer.

## 4. Self-Revision Checklist

Before delivering the final value asset, you MUST audit your draft against this checklist:

- [ ] **Declarative Check:** Does the asset teach *about* the concept without giving away the step-by-step implementation (unless it's a narrow Quick Win)?
- [ ] **Audience Alignment:** Is the core desire or objection pulled directly from `audience_intelligence.md`?
- [ ] **Mechanism Integration:** Is the Unique Mechanism positioned as the logical solution to the root problem?
- [ ] **No Forced Negatives:** Has the copy avoided the "It's not X, it's Y" pattern entirely?
- [ ] **Voice & Tone:** Does the copy match the specific voice fingerprint defined in `voice_model.md`?
- [ ] **Banned Patterns:** Have you scanned for and removed all patterns listed in `banned_patterns.md`?
- [ ] **Clear CTA:** Does the asset tell the prospect exactly what to do next?
